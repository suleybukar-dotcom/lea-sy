Photography

Montréal, seen
slowly.

Personal photography made around Montréal, streets, cafés, light, rain, and the quiet moments between everything else. Shot on a Sony α7C with a 35mm.

Photography as a way
of paying attention.

I came back to photography in the autumn of 2024, after almost two years of not really seeing things. I'd been in a period of overwork, head down, screen-forward, moving fast. When I finally slowed down, I bought a camera and started walking the city without a destination.

Most of what I shoot is mundane: a café counter at 8am, rain on a window, a stranger crossing an intersection. I'm not looking for extraordinary moments. I'm looking for ordinary ones that deserve a second look.

These are all taken in and around Montréal, mostly in Le Plateau, Mile End, Rosemont, and the Old Port. Shot in colour, occasionally converted to black and white when the light asks for it.

Equipment

What I shoot with.

Sony α7C full-frame mirrorless · 35mm f/1.8 prime (my main) · 50mm f/1.4 for portraits · edited in Adobe Lightroom. Everything is shot in RAW and processed with a fairly restrained hand. I'm not big on heavy presets.
