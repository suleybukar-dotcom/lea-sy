Where I'm from, and where I ended up.

I was born in France to Senegalese parents who had moved to Paris in the late nineties. I grew up in the southern suburbs, Créteil mostly, then the 12th arrondissement once my parents found more stability. My father worked in accounting; my mother was a nurse for twenty years. Education was never presented as a choice. It was the plan, the conversation that ended every dinner, the thread that held everything together.

I did my classes préparatoires at a lycée in Paris, two years that I can only describe as the most intellectually demanding and personally isolating period of my life. I emerged with a strong work ethic, a mild distrust of perfectionism, and a place at HEC Paris. I studied marketing there from 2018 to 2021, and it was the first time I felt genuinely at home in a subject. The intersection of data, storytelling, and human behaviour, that's where I've lived professionally ever since.

"I spent a lot of those years running on discipline alone. I didn't know yet how to want things for myself."

I graduated from HEC in 2021 and moved to Montréal two months later. It wasn't a dramatic decision, more of a quiet conviction that I needed to build something somewhere that was entirely mine. No family fifteen minutes away, no inherited social map. I arrived in September with two suitcases and a job offer from a small marketing agency in Griffintown that I'd found through a LinkedIn connection during my final semester.

The first year was harder than I'd admitted to anyone. Montréal winters are nothing like Paris winters. I worked more than I should have, spent too many Sundays alone, and told myself I was fine in the way you do when the alternative is acknowledging that you're not. But I was also building something real, professionally, financially, emotionally. I changed jobs twice in three years, both times intentionally. I started feeling, slowly, like I was choosing my life rather than just surviving it.

Where I am now.

I'm 26. I live alone in a two-and-a-half in the Plateau that I've slowly made feel like mine, prints on the walls, plants I've kept alive for over a year, a kitchen I actually cook in. I work as a Digital Marketing Specialist at Mäk Creative Studio, a team I like and respect. I go to pilates on Tuesdays and Thursdays and swim at the Rosemont pool on weekends when I remember to book a lane in time.

Photography came back into my life in a way I didn't expect. I'd been taking casual phone photos for years, but last autumn I bought a Sony mirrorless camera with money I'd been setting aside for months, and something shifted. I started noticing things differently, the way light falls on wet pavement, the particular grey of a Montréal March sky, strangers at café tables. It became a practice, not a hobby. Something I do for myself, not for an audience.

This website is a small archive of that. The work I do professionally, the things I think about, the photos I take, the routines that keep me steady. I'm not trying to be a brand. I'm just a person who went through a lot of hard, unglamorous years and came out the other side with a quiet life she's actually grateful for.

What I care about.

01
Intentionality

I don't want more. I want the right things. Work that's meaningful, routines that sustain me, relationships that are real. I try to make decisions slowly and live with them honestly.

02
Craft

Whether it's a campaign strategy, a journal entry, or a photograph, I care about doing the thing properly. Not perfectly, but properly. There's a difference, and I think it matters.

03
Rest as a discipline

After years of overworking out of necessity, rest became something I had to consciously learn. I'm still learning. But I believe that sustainable output requires real recovery, not just shorter days.

04
Honesty

I'm not interested in performing a version of my life that doesn't exist. The polished posts, the curated captions, I understand the appeal, but I try to stay closer to what's actually true.

05
Community

I didn't grow up here, which means I've had to build a community from scratch. That's made me take it more seriously. The people I've chosen to keep close are the foundation of everything.

06
Curiosity

I want to keep being the kind of person who reads widely, asks real questions, and stays genuinely interested in the world. I think that's the most useful thing I can cultivate.
